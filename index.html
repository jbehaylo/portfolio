Visualize Salesforce Data
>I spent 20 years managing the grant-making process for the Open Society Foundations, during which time we became a more data-driven organization, with key functions and business processes enabled by cloud-based systems, especially on the Salesforce platform. Part of my role was to help colleagues and leadership make effective use of all the data we were collecting as part of our paperless grant process. This first piece, “OSF Support of Grassroots/Small NGOs”, tests a core belief about what made us special as a funder – our support of grassroots and small nonprofits around the world that do not have access to Global North funders. The first published version had a single page centered on the stacked area chart with a few supporting tables and very little narrative. In revising it, I ended up expanding to the 2-page story here to split out an answer to the basic question at a global level before diving into the global regions data that had been at the center of version one. The data was exported from Salesforce to CSV, with additional fields appended using Excel formulas.
Extract and Visualize Data from Proprietary Systems
“ Celebrities Giving Back…” developed from my interest in working with IRS tax return data for private foundations and seeking a story that would interest a general audience. I relied on the Candid Foundation Directory Online interface, which turned out to be a very limiting experience. Candid does not allow you to download large datasets, so I needed to pick a few foundations with a limited number of records and export to Excel in small batches. Then I compiled all of these exports together into a master file and used xlookups and other advanced Excel formulas to back-fill some missing data elements.
Visualize Data from Publicly Available Datasets
The third piece here, “What’s So Great About the U.S. Healthcare System?”, relied on pulling public health data from the OECD website, and doing extensive data manipulation in Tableau Prep Builder and using Excel formulas. An early decision was to go with a story format rather than a dashboard, in order to present the health expenditure and public health indicator data on distinct pages. There were many health indicators and other data points to consider in deciding which would be most compelling for a general audience.
